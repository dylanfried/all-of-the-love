From Kim Wicklund

Happy 70th birthday, Bob! It is a perfect Pacific Northwest morning. Cool, fresh air being warmed by the rising sun. Blue skies dotted with an occasional wispy cloud. Birds twittering. The magnificent magnolia tossing a random petal at me and Vesper, who is by my side. It's a fitting morning to be reflecting on YOU and the many perfect mornings we've shared in various places on the planet: a private lookout over Mulley Canyon, a very sandy bend on the Green River, an oasis in the Moroccan desert, an Icelandic picnic table frequented by frolicking foxes, a mountain top in the Dolomites that is truly "a loooong way" from whatever we wanted it to be far from, and in your restful backyard where you have brought me many lattes to start the day. We have shared so many fabulous adventures together. Those times were enriched because they were infused with your essence: your kindness and incredibly generous spirit, your curiosity about everything and vast range of knowledge, your sense of adventure and desire to be untethered, your unique ability to be completely present and focused on whoever it is you are with, your creativity and productivity, your balance of seriousness with utter wackiness, your love of music and desire to bring it with you wherever you go. You live life with arms wide open, always ready to embrace it. On a personal level, you have an especially beautiful way of making me feel heard and validated – you are such a good listener! Throughout your 70 trips around the sun, I can only imagine how many people you've helped feel heard, made laugh, encouraged to sing, helped with a project, inspired to embrace all that life offers. You are a special one, Bob, and I thank the Universe for bringing such a beautiful soul into my life. May you feel loved and adored as you move into this next year of your wonderful life.

Love you tons,
Kim

From Sherrie

Bob,

It is hard to believe that you are turning 70. My memories of you as a little boy are so vivid. You were Karen's and my little toy. We'd play choo choo around the house with a cloth diaper and you would hold onto it as we ran around the house saying choo choo. You were always smiling and laughing. I remember when you came home after getting your stomach pumped because you managed to use an oven rack to pull the box of rat poisoning out from under the oven. You came home smiling while sucking on a lollipop. You were always so good natured. I wonder if your experiences in the emergency room as a kid lit the spark that ultimately led you to become a doctor.

As we grew up with almost six years between us, I felt the age difference and we weren't close. Now I sometimes feel badly that I really didn't get to know you then. On the other hand, now I sometimes feel that you are the "elder" of the family and that I can depend on you for your honesty, your incredible medical knowledge, your perceptive questions and your lack of judgement. I feel so lucky to have a brother like you who is easy to work with, generous, kind, silly, adventurous, and cares about being part of our family.

I've included a photo that I found that you may not have. It is of Karen, grandma Julia, aunt Edith, Mom and Aunt Claribel and me. I'm not sure how old you were. I've also included a photo of our Family Tree that mom put together for me that I thought you may like.

Wishing you many more adventures and incredible experiences as you step into your 70's. I'll be thinking about you on your birthday feeling like I am so lucky to have a brother like you. Thank you for always being there. I'll also be thinking about how lucky we were to have the parents we did who made us into the people we are. I recently found the letter that Karen, you and I wrote to mom and dad on their 50th anniversary. The following is a quote from it:

"We're who we are because of who you are: your stability, strength and how you loved us. You taught us to always be honest, self-reliant, fair and productive."

I think we have passed the same traits on to our children. HAPPY BIRTHDAY!!!!

Love,
Sherrie

From Andy and Betsy

All of the stories I have about Bob…at least the stories I have that I will share, involve Bob the musician and Bob the hero!

Betsy and I have been lucky enough to travel many times with Bob and almost always he has brought his guitar. Music is always welcome, especially when it is loud enough to cover up our singing! But, alas, we tend to really belt it out when he plays Wagonwheel!

Once when in the Caribbean sailing, we took an afternoon off to tour the island of Nevis. Upon returning to the boat, I took the helm and promptly stated steering for the rocky shore. Then Landman started yelling at me and grabbed the wheel when he realized we had lost our steering!! Yikes!! The rocks crept closer until Bob Fried remembered we had an emergency tiller, which he promptly retrieved, fitted into the mechanism and guided us away from the rocks.

Another time, again sailing, again in the Caribbean, one of the crew fouled a line and had it not been for Bob Fried remembering an obscure knot, the Rolling Hitch, we would have been in quite the pickle.

It seems that Bob F pays attention to the safety walkthrus and knot lectures!!

I do remember a couple of nights in the BVIs when we were taking a couple of sailing courses. Three of us shared a room with two king beds. Andy won the toss and got a bed to himself while the two Bobs slept in the other bed. Rumor has it that one of the two Bobs snores.

When we think of Bob Fried, we always focus on what a sweet, caring person he is. We are lucky he is in our life!

Andy and Betsy

To Bob on the occasion of his 70th birthday

My dear friend Bob –

You've been a constant presence in my life. Such a gift to have a friendship with you spanning nearly four decades (our 40th meeting anniversary is coming up next year!)

When I think about you, so many adjectives come to mind. In no particular order:

Curious: You explore so many topics, making conversations an expanding experience when you share what you are learning and thinking about. Also curious about people – whether about what's going on in the lives of those close to you or people you are meeting for the first time.

Connecting: I love our conversations where we go deep so quickly – such rewarding and meaningful moments. No pretense, no walls – just being ourselves.

Gregarious: You have a knack for connecting with all sorts of people, finding common ground, drawing out their life experiences. You are in the moment, present, enthusiastic, excited when making a connection. This is one of the things that makes it great to travel with you – you connect so easily with strangers and that enriches the experience.

Generous: You are so ready to share your time and knowledge to help out friends and family. You are so there for your people. It's inspired me, helped me look at myself to see how I can be better at this. What you've done for your parents, for Karen, for Pam's family with the land resolution, and so much more. How you've cared for your community and offered support to people in difficult circumstances.

Searching: You have never stopped seeking deeper understanding of yourself, of how you fit into the cosmos – I love hearing about your musings and insights whether spiritual, scientific, personal. Gets me to ask the questions.

Playful: I love how we can go to silly and goofy places – just stop being tooooo serious. Not sure I can find words to describe those accents.

Adventurous: Ready to explore and push the limits and learn new skills – sailing, canyoneering, pursuing the High Route – you're all in.

Nurturing and loving: Boy, do you have a big heart! I love basking in the warmth of your friendship, as do so many.

Creative: I love listening to you play the guitar, see what you've been up to with the welding and metal work.

And so many great memories of times with you – and not just any times but some of the best ones.

Our 40 year exploration of the Sierras, starting with the hike on the JMT in medical school where you introduced me to this magical range of light. The trip had everything from crisp and beautiful days with the granite gleaming in the unique crystalline Sierra light to the snow and cold that taught me that the mountains are always unpredictable and indifferent. And since then, so many other trips on the High Route, Mitre Basin, with the kids. Those High Route trips – wondering how we are going to get from here to there, but knowing that with you, we will do it. Looking at the wilderness before us and somehow finding a way forward together – and helping each other find ways forward in other realms as our lives have unfolded. Knowing that we are there to catch each other if we fall. Sitting together, in silence, absorbing the grandeur, the deep time of the mountains.

And then there are the Winds – great explorations. Being stuck in that storm in the weird single wall tent as the snow came down and the wind howled and we bailed out.

And the hikes in the Cascades – The Alpine Lakes High Route with great views, nasty leg cramps, granite basins holding sky blue lakes framed with the walls of Overcoat, Chimney and Summit Chief.

And Utah – exploring so many canyons over the years, sometimes the two of us, sometimes the four of us. Tracking the kivas of the ancient ones, being enveloped in soaring walls, shimmying through slot canyons, wading through pools tucked deep in canyon depths, hiking the open mesas, floating down the Green and Colorado.

And the Dolomites – getting to share these dramatic, moody mountains with you, Pam and Kim, sunny days seeing forever, misty days shrouding the peaks and hiding the basins.

And our trips together to Iceland (wading through icy streams to get to the impossibly verdant slope of Hornstrandir, tracking down puffins, not letting a little Covid get in the way) and Morocco (the huge waves of golden and ochre sand frozen in the moment as we clambered up their sides, deep and stark canyons, towns with crazy towers and walls of mud and sand).

Looking forward to many more adventures and quieter times both!

I am so glad to have you in my life.

Love,
Jim
